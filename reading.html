Notes on Blindness
A documentary, dramatic adaptation of writer John Hulls descent into blindness.
He starts to make a diary on audio cassettes as an outlet for the immense
change he is going through.

The film has three chapters; the first is visual memory and the construction of
self. The second is the struggle with acceptance, wondering whether peace can
truly be found in blindness and the third is a celebration of sensations which is a
part of the piece presented as the reading.
"others cannot be seen or held in their eyes anymore"
The tapes record grief but also renewal and a new sense of vision. I think it
is very hard to lose sight, after having it, being able to see, and that being
taken away feels like a deeper loss than not having sight at all.
"cognition is beautiful, its beautiful to know"
The cinematography adds so much depth to the narration. The soft blur, the tactile
sound design, the attention to textural detail makes everything so visceral, it feels
like you are going along with the experience that is being narrated and are present.
It goes to show not only how important visuals are to design/cinema but sound is something
that allows for space and dimension to be created. Another aspect narrated in the film itself.
"is the loss of the image of the face tied to the loss of self; you start to feel like
a spirit in existence"
"rain is like a continuous blanket of differentiated but
specialised sound. Uninterrupted. If only there was something
like rain falling inside - so the room would take dimension"
You see from the narration that John Hull functions better with familiarity,
his memory. Do you think people require predictability and stability to function
normally or at their best? How does a fixed and ever changing environment affect
your abilties, and in what ways?

Even though the brain is such an adapting organ we seem
to live and thrive in familiarity.

Do you think in design familiarity and a stable nature are too prominent in terms of
designing for the able, such as designing for peple with vision. And we should be
venturing out of that stability and make more for the unfamiliar, the unable? Because
isn't that a part of design, to help, solve, create?
